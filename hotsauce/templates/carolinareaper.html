Carolina Reaper

The Carolina Reaper, originally named HP22B, is a variety of the Capsicum chinense plant. In 2013, it was named the hottest chili in the world by Guinness World Records. The pepper is a crossbreed between a ghost pepper (previously the world's hottest pepper) and a red habanero. It was bred by "Smokin" Ed Currie, proprietor of the PuckerButt Pepper Company in South Carolina. The name of the pepper come from the shape of its tail. On average, the pepper rates at 1,569,300 Scoville Heat Units (SHU). The hottest individual pepper was measured at 2.2 million SHU. The current record for number of carolina reapers eaten in a minute is 22.
